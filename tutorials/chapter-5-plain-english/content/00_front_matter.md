# About this book

This is a friendly retelling of Chapter 5 of *AI-Powered Search*. The original chapter is technical, careful, and full of the working code you'd need to build the systems it describes. That's exactly what its intended audience — search engineers — wants from it.

This version is written for a different reader. Someone who's curious about how modern search works, but doesn't write code for a living. Someone who can follow a diagram, understands what an analogy is asking them to do, and would rather read a paragraph than a code listing.

If you're a product manager, an analyst, a designer, an engineer in another part of the stack — this is for you. You'll come away from this chapter with a working mental model of how a search engine can be turned into a knowledge graph, why anyone would want to do that, and what kinds of problems that solves. You won't come away knowing how to write the code yourself, and that's fine.

## What you'll find in this chapter

Five sections, each building on the last:

1. **Knowledge graphs: connecting the dots.** What a knowledge graph is, the two pieces every graph is built from, and the three ways people put them together.

2. **Your search engine already knows more than you think.** A surprising claim — the search engine you may already have is secretly a graph database — and how to see that for yourself.

3. **Teaching a computer to read between the lines.** Two ways to pull facts out of plain text and put them into a knowledge graph.

4. **Finding hidden connections in your words.** The heart of the chapter. Watch a search engine discover that *advil*, *motrin*, and *aleve* are related — without anyone telling it.

5. **Putting it all together: smarter search.** How the pieces fit, and a look at what they let you build.

You'll see a sprinkle of callouts throughout:

> **Tip.** Practical advice you can use right away.

> **Warning.** Things that sound right but get people in trouble.

> **Technical Stuff.** Skippable. For the curious; not needed to follow the argument.

> **Remember.** A one-line takeaway worth holding on to.

The chapter ends with a glossary of every term we'll introduce, and a one-page cheat sheet you can keep nearby.

Ready when you are.
